St Alban's Boys' Home, Murrurundi (1942 - 1952)

  • Full page

Alternative Names

St Albans Boys' Home, at Haydon Street Murrurundi, was founded in 1942. St Alban's, also known as Morpeth Home for Children, had moved from its original site at Morpeth. St Alban's Murrurundi was run by the Church Army until 1948 and then by the Anglican Diocese of Newcastle.

Details

The Muswellbrook Chronicle reported in May 1942 that the St Alban's Boys Home was moving to Murrurundi, to a property Myo'a in Haydon Street that had been bought by the Diocese of Newcastle:

Useful additions and alterations have been carried out, including two large dormitories and glassed-in verandahs. Rooms have been enlarged, making it a comfortable and suitable home for the boys and staff. There are 35 boys in the home. They are in charge of Captain and Mrs Roberts. Later the grounds will be improved by the planting of trees and shrubs.

The home was officially opened and blessed by the Bishop of Newcastle. The Bishop was accompanied by Archdeacon Wood and the Rev M Redman.

The Anglican Diocese of Newcastle assumed the management of the home in 1948. Rev AR McFarland was priest-in-charge of St Albans Home for Boys at Murrurundi from 1948 until 1950.

The Murrurundi property proved inadequate for the needs of the Home, and it was moved in 1952 to 'Winncourt', Mayfield, which had formerly been St Elizabeth's Girls' Home.
